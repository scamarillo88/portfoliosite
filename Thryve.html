Timeline

Process

For this project, I followed a full design process—starting with user research and early sketches, then building out wireframes, testing, and refining the UI. My goal was to create something clear, approachable, and useful for everyday financial habits.

Vision and User Research

Research

I focused on users like young professionals and freelancers who wanted to feel more in control of their money. The research showed a strong need for something simple that helps people track their spending and save toward goals—without stress or confusion.

Userflow and Low Fidelity Wireframes

Ideation

I started sketching out features that would make the experience feel encouraging and easy to follow. Goal-based savings, visual progress tracking, and helpful tips were core ideas from the beginning.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Wireframes

I created low- and mid-fidelity wireframes to map out the app’s main features—like the dashboard, savings goals, and expenses. I made updates based on feedback, including layout tweaks, icon changes, and spacing improvements for better clarity.

Testing and Brand Persona

Prototype

I built a clickable prototype so users could walk through key actions like adding a goal or logging expenses. Feedback helped guide changes like button sizing, text updates, and adjusting the navigation to feel more natural.

Style Guide

Design

The visual style is soft, clean, and supportive. I used a green primary color to reflect growth and guidance, paired with Poppins for a modern, easy-to-read look. The brand is meant to feel friendly but still professional.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Conclusion

Thryve is about making saving feel possible. It breaks things down into simple steps so people can make smart money choices without getting overwhelmed. It’s built to support real goals—like that trip you’ve been planning or the emergency fund you’ve been putting off.
